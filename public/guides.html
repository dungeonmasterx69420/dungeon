All Devices
How to Watch
Finding shows, picking the right stream, and what to click
▼
Step 1 — Search & Open a Title
  • 1
    Go to web.stremio.com and sign in with your credentials.
  • 2
    Use the search bar to find a movie or show. Tap or click on the result to open its detail page — you'll see a summary, cast, and ratings.
  • 3
    For TV shows, tap the season selector in the middle of the screen to choose the season you want to watch. Then scroll down and select an episode.
    The season selector looks like a pill button that says "Season 1", "Season 2" etc. Tap it to open a dropdown with all available seasons.
Step 2 — Picking a Stream
  • 1
    Once you tap an episode or movie, Stremio shows a list of available streams. Each row shows the source on the left and file details on the right — resolution, size, audio format, and language flags.
  • 2
    The entries labeled [TB⚡] StremThru Torz are your primary streams — these are the ones connected to your Dungeon account. Always choose one of these.
    Look for [TB⚡] on the left side of each row. These are pre-cached and ready to play instantly.
  • 3
    Tap the stream you want to play. The first stream in the list is usually the highest quality — but read on before picking 4K.
Choosing the Right Quality
  • !
    On mobile — start with 1080p, not 4K.
    Most phone screens cap out at 1080p — even high-end phones rarely render 4K video correctly. More importantly, 4K (2160p) files are enormous — often 14–181 GB — and require significantly more processing power and bandwidth to decode. On mobile these streams frequently stutter, buffer, or refuse to play entirely. Start with a 1080p stream and only try 4K if you're casting to a capable TV.
  • 1
    Look at the resolution label on the left side of each stream row. You'll see 2160p (4K) and 1080p options. On mobile, scroll past the 2160p entries and pick the first 1080p one.
  • 2
    On the right side of each row you'll see the file size — something like 1.72 GB or 2.35 GB. A 1080p WEB-DL around 1–3 GB is ideal for mobile — fast to start and smooth to play.
  • 3
    If you see BluRay REMUX or sizes like 16 GB, 58 GB, or 181 GB — those are archival-quality files for home theater setups. Skip these on mobile and on slower internet connections.
  • 4
    The flag icons at the bottom of each row show available subtitle/audio languages. 🇬🇧 = English. Look for English if you need subtitles or dubbed audio.
Quick Reference
  • ✓
    Good on mobile: 1080p · WEB-DL · 1–4 GB · 🇬🇧 flag
  • ✓
    Good on TV / desktop: 2160p · BluRay · any size
  • ✗
    Avoid on mobile: 2160p · BluRay REMUX · 14 GB+ files
If a stream won't play or buffers
Go back and try a different stream — pick one with a smaller file size or lower resolution. If nothing plays, the content may not be cached yet. Wait a few minutes and try again, or try a WEB-DL version instead of BluRay.

iPhone & iPad
iOS Setup
Add to home screen + Outplayer for playback
▼
Standard Method
  • 1
    Open Safari on your device and go to web.stremio.com.
    Must use Safari — other browsers don't support Add to Home Screen.
  • 2
    Tap the Share icon at the bottom of the screen, then tap Add to Home Screen. Name it "Stremio" and tap Add.
  • 3
    Open the app from your home screen and sign in with your Stremio credentials. Signing in ensures your addons and settings sync properly.
  • 4
    Go to the App Store and download Outplayer — it's free. This lets Stremio open stream links in a proper video player.
  • 5
    In Stremio Web, go to Settings → Advanced and set the external player to Outplayer.
    Once set, tapping any stream will open automatically in Outplayer.
For Advanced Users — Sideload the Full App
The official Stremio IPA is available at stremio.com/downloads. Use AltStore or Sideloadly to install it — no jailbreak required. Free Apple IDs require re-signing every 7 days. On iOS 14–17, TrollStore installs it permanently.

Fire TV · Android TV · Google TV
TV Setup
App store or sideload via Downloader
▼
Try the App Store First
  • 1
    Open the App Store / Play Store / Amazon Appstore on your TV and search for Stremio. If it appears, install it and you're done.
    Stremio is available on some Fire TV and Android TV devices. Try this first before using Downloader.
If Not in the Store — Use Downloader
  • 1
    Install the Downloader app by AFTVnews from your TV's app store. It's free and available on Fire TV and Android TV.
  • 2
    Enable unknown app installs. On Fire TV: Settings → My Fire TV → Developer Options → Install Unknown Apps → enable Downloader. On Android TV: Settings → Apps → Downloader → enable Unknown Sources.
  • 3
    Open Downloader, select the URL field, and type stremio.com, then press Go. This loads Stremio's official download page.
    Always use the official site — it's the safest source for the APK.
  • 4
    Tap Download for Android TV / Fire TV. Once downloaded, tap Install.
  • 5
    Open Stremio and sign in with your credentials. You can scan the QR code on screen with your phone to log in quickly.

Windows & Mac
Desktop Setup
Download the app or use Stremio in your browser
▼
Option 1 — Download the Desktop App (Recommended)
  • 1
    Go to stremio.com/downloads and download the version for your OS — Windows or macOS.
  • 2
    Run the installer and open Stremio once it's done.
  • 3
    Sign in with your Stremio credentials. Your addons and library sync automatically.
Option 2 — Use Stremio Web (No Install)
  • 1
    Open any browser and go to web.stremio.com.
  • 2
    Sign in with your credentials — everything works the same as the desktop app, right in your browser. No download needed.
Which should I use?
The desktop app handles high-quality streams more reliably. The web version is great if you're on a work computer or don't want to install anything.

Android Phone & Tablet
Android Setup
Play Store app or add web app to home screen
▼
Option 1 — Download from the Play Store (Recommended)
  • 1
    Open the Google Play Store and search for Stremio. Look for the official app — it has a purple icon with a white streaming logo.
    This is the Stremio icon — make sure you're downloading the right app.
  • 2
    Tap Install and open the app once it's downloaded.
  • 3
    Sign in with your Stremio credentials. Your addons and library sync automatically.
Option 2 — Add Stremio Web to Home Screen
  • 1
    Open Chrome on your Android device and go to web.stremio.com. Sign in with your credentials.
  • 2
    Tap the three-dot menu (⋮) in the top-right corner of Chrome, then tap Add to Home screen.
  • 3
    Name it "Stremio" and tap Add. It opens full-screen like a native app.
  • 4
    For the best playback, go to Settings → Advanced inside Stremio Web and set an external player like MX Player or VLC.
    Both are free on the Play Store. MX Player handles the most formats.
